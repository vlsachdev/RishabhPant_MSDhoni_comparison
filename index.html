Is Rishabh Pant better Test player than MS Dhoni?

A quick comparison between the current wicketkeeper bastman with early Test career statistics of India's best wicketkeeper captain MS Dhoni

by Vineet Sachdev

Rishabh Pant, the Indian cricket player has been in news lately due to his performance in Test matches. The wicketkeeper batman scored a century in India’s last Test match against England in early July. Pant has been talked of as a long-term successor to MS Dhoni, India’s most successful wicketkeeper captain in many media reports.

We look at the performance of the young player in Test matches and see how his numbers compare to MS Dhoni’s initial Test match career.Dhoni had retired from Test cricket in 2014 after playing 90 matches. The analysis is based on the performance for both players during their initial 31 matches in their respective careers since Pant has played only 31 tests till date.

Pant as batsman

A comparison of batting numbers shows that Rishabh Pant has better numbers than Dhoni’s initial career statistics. Since the beginning of his Test career in 2018, Pant has scored 2123 runs in 53 outings. In comparison Dhoni had scored a total of 1587 runs in his 50 innings. While Dhoni had an average of 36.07, Rishabh Pants’ average has been 43.33. The young batsmans' strike rate of 71.84 in Test matches is also better than Dhoni (65.07) in these many matches.

Batting numbers also shows that after facing higher number of balls (greater than 100) at the crease, Pants’ score mostly remains close to 100 or above while in comparison Dhoni’s score stayed mostly less than 100.
